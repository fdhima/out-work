# OutWork — Instagram & TikTok Content Strategy

**Goal:** Grow reach → drive App Store downloads
**Target Audience:** Remote workers, digital nomads, freelancers

---

## Content Pillars

### 1. "Work From Anywhere" Lifestyle
*Shareable.* Aspirational content showing beautiful, productive workspaces. Taps into the remote work identity people want to project.

### 2. Hidden Gems & Local Discoveries
*Shareable + searchable.* Spotlights specific cafes, coworking spaces, and libraries. Creates a sense of discovery and FOMO.

### 3. Remote Worker Life (Relatable & Funny)
*Shareable.* Humor-driven content about WFH struggles, bad WiFi, loud cafe neighbors — problems OutWork solves. High virality potential on TikTok.

### 4. Productivity & Work Setup Tips
*Searchable (TikTok SEO).* Practical tips for being productive outside the home. Naturally leads to "find a spot with OutWork."

### 5. Community Spotlights (UGC)
*Trust-building.* Repost user-submitted venues and reviews. Shows real people using the app.

---

## Platform Strategy

### TikTok — Primary Growth Engine

TikTok's algorithm allows zero-follower accounts to go viral. Prioritize this platform first.

**Content formats:**
- **"Best spots to work in [City]"** — Walk-through videos of cafes/coworking spaces. High search intent. Add text overlay with venue details + "Find more on OutWork."
- **"POV: Finding the perfect cafe to work from"** — Relatable storytelling. Hook: bad experience → app solves it.
- **"Rating work cafes so you don't have to"** — Review-style content. Entertaining, shareable, drives curiosity about the app.
- **"Remote work red flags"** — Humor content (bad WiFi, crying baby next to you, 1 outlet for 20 people). Ends with "That's why I use OutWork."
- **Duet/Stitch hooks** — Invite users to duet their best workspace finds. Free UGC.

**Posting cadence:** 5–7x per week. Consistency is critical in TikTok's algorithm.

**TikTok SEO:** Include searchable phrases in captions and spoken audio — "best cafe to work from," "coworking spaces near me," "remote work spots."

---

### Instagram — Community & Brand Authority

Use Instagram to build a loyal community and brand identity that TikTok discovery converts into.

**Content formats:**
- **Reels** (repurposed from TikTok, slightly edited): Same video content, trimmed for Instagram's pacing.
- **Carousels** — "Top 5 work cafes in [City]" — high save rate = high reach. Saves are Instagram's strongest engagement signal.
- **Stories** — Behind-the-scenes of venue scouting, polls ("Hot desk or private office?"), "Rate this spot" stickers.
- **UGC Reposts** — Share user-submitted photos of venues they found on OutWork. Tag them.
- **Place Highlight Series** — One venue per week, featured in a consistent visual format (branded template). Encourages venue owners to share too.

**Posting cadence:** 4–5x per week (3 Reels + 1–2 carousels/static).

**Profile optimization:**
- Bio: "Find the perfect spot to work from ☕ | Remote workers & digital nomads | Download the app ↓"
- Link in bio → direct App Store link
- Story Highlights: "Spots," "Reviews," "Tips," "How It Works"

## Growth Tactics

### App Store Download CTAs
- TikTok: End every video with "Link in bio — find your spot" or show the app UI for 3–5 seconds.
- Instagram: Every carousel last slide = "Download OutWork → link in bio."
- Use Smart Links (Linktree or similar) to route to iOS App Store + Android Play Store.

### Location Hashtag Strategy

Instagram hashtags (mix per post):
- Niche: `#remotework` `#digitalnomad` `#workfromcafe` `#coworkingspace`
- Location: `#[CityName]cafes` `#workin[City]`
- Lifestyle: `#wfh` `#laptoplifestyle` `#cafevibes`

TikTok hashtags: `#remotework` `#wfhlife` `#digitalnomad` `#cafehopping` `#workfromcafe`

### Venue Partnership Outreach
- DM cafes and coworking spaces: "We featured your spot on OutWork — here's the post, feel free to share!" Many will reshare to their own audiences, giving free distribution.
- Offer verified/featured venue badges in exchange for a post tagging OutWork.

### Creator Seeding (Low Cost)
- Find micro-influencers (5K–50K followers) in the remote work / digital nomad niche.
- Offer early app access or a featured venue listing in exchange for an honest video.
- Target travel + productivity creators — their audiences are your exact ICP.

## Content Production Tips

Since this is likely a small team:
1. **Batch film** venue visits — capture enough footage for 4–5 videos per location visit.
2. **Repurpose everything** — one venue visit = TikTok video + Instagram Reel + carousel + 2 Stories.
3. **Use CapCut** for TikTok editing (free, fast, trending templates built in).
4. **Recruit power users** early — invite your most active app users to contribute content. Give them credit.
5. **Film the app in use** — show the map, the filtering, the reviews. Demystifies the product and drives downloads.

## Content Without a Physical Venue                                              
                                
### Screen-Based Content (Your Best Option)
                                                                              
Film your phone/screen showing the app in action. This works extremely well on
  TikTok:                                                                      
- "I found a hidden gem cafe 3 hours from me using this app"                  
- "Rating workspaces I'd love to try" — scrolling through places on the map   
- "How I plan my next workcation using OutWork"                            
                                                                              
No travel needed. Shows the product directly.                                 
                                                                              
### Repost & Curate User Content                                               
                                                                              
Ask early users or people in cities to submit venue photos/videos in exchange 
for a feature shoutout. You become the editor, not the filmmaker.
                                                                              
### "Remote Work Tips" Content                                                 
  
Purely talking-head or text-on-screen videos work great:                      
- "5 things to check before working from a cafe"
- "Why I stopped working from home"                                           
- "What I look for in a work-from-cafe spot"
                                                                              
These are opinion/advice content — no venue needed.                           
                                                                              
### Plan a Monthly Content Trip                                                
                                                                              
Once a month, travel to the nearest city for a half-day. Film 8–10 venues in  
one go. That's enough content for 2–3 weeks across both platforms.
                                                                              
### Leverage Other Creators' Content                                           
  
- Find videos of cafes/coworking spaces on TikTok or YouTube and Stitch/Duet  
them with your commentary: "This is exactly the kind of spot you can find on
OutWork."                                                                     
- This also piggybacks on the original video's existing traffic.
                                                                              
### User-Generated Content (UGC) Campaigns                                     
                                                                              
Run a challenge: "Show us your OutWork spot" — ask users to post their        
workspace found via the app and tag you. Repost the best ones. Your audience
builds the content library for you.                                           
                
---
Bottom line: The most viral content on TikTok right now is talking-head and
screen-recording content, not cinematic venue tours. Your village location is 
not a blocker — the app itself is the content.
